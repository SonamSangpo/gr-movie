Depicting the legendary story of ‘The Second Buddha’ who brought the teachings to Tibet and introduced Vajrayana to the world, The Life of Guru Rinpoche is a 12 hour epic produced at Namdrolling Monastery in the 90’s under the auspices of His Holiness Penor Rinoche. It has been translated and remastered by Venerable Lama Dondrup Dorje, with the blessings of HH Penor Rinpoche, in order to bring the inspiring story to a wider audience.

About the Film

  • The Project

    ‘The Life of Guru Rinpoche’ started out as an epic 12 part stage play for the local Tibetan communities, performed solely by the monks of Namdrolling Monastery in the 1990s. With limited budget and technology available, additional location shots and computer generated special effects were added and the play was adapted into a VHS release in order to be shared with a wider audience.

    HH Penor Rinpoche, who personally directed and oversaw the production of the film, wanted it to be made available to as many beings as possible, however due to the profound teachings involved in the dialogue, many were reluctant to take on such a monumental task. His Holiness then asked his long term disciple Venerable Lama Dondrup Dorje to begin the process of translating the feature into English.

    Venerable Lama Dondrup Dorje initially oversaw the translation project, which involved hundreds of translators and proof readers. During the process of adding subtitles, the footage was cleaned up, colour corrected and fit to a widescreen format. Due to being VHS recorded, some of the quality needed significant remastering work before it was ready for re-distribution. During this time, Venerable Lama Dondrup Dorje set about investigating the possibility of enhancing the special effects to make it more appealing to a modern audience. A trailer was produced and shown to HH Penor Rinpoche, who was delighted with the efforts and gave his blessings for Venerable Lama Dondrup Dorje to begin the process of completely remastering the film and overseeing the translation into multiple languages.

    The Process

  • The Process

    The footage was filmed originally as a stage play at Namdrolling Monastery in front of painted cloth backgrounds, with Tulkus, monks and Lamas portraying all the roles to a live audience. It was further enhanced with the addition of scenes filmed on location in India, and home made special effects and post production.

    Venerable Lama Dondrup Dorje oversaw the process of translation with Shedra students in the Nyagyur Nyingma Institute at Namdrolling Monastery and then checked through by educated lamas, which took several years alone due to the profound teachings contained in the script. The Pathgate Institute Editorial Committee then digitised the footage, added the subtitles, colour corrected and reformatted to widescreen. This was the initial work completed.

    With exclusive access to original footage, Pathgate Institute were able to enhance some or the special effects scenes. This led onto fully recreating some scenes, using cutting-edge techniques. Special effects are being added to existing scenes. Students have travelled to shoot footage in various countries using drone and time-lapse photography. We are meticulously applying a process called rotoscoping in order to extract actors from scenes, frame by frame, from the painted on clothes backgrounds and place them into digitally enhanced scenes. The soundtrack has been enhanced and complimentary scores added. Some entirely new scenes have been created to fill in parts of the story. Animated cut scenes are being produced to introduce each chapter. Creating a green-screen studio and props and costumes, we are able to introduce new characters or recreate some scenes in their entirety. And an international group of translators are working hard to provide subtitles in Chinese, German, Italian, Greek, Vietnamese and Romanian.

    The Project The Legend

  • The Legend

    Who is Guru Rinpoche?

    Guru Rinpoche (Precious Guru), in his emanation as Padmasambhava (the Lotus-Born One), was born under miraculous circumstances in Northern Kashmir in the 8th century C.E. His birth was foretold by the Buddha Shakyamuni who referred to Guru Rinpoche as a ‘Buddha of Extraordinary Skill’.

    Padmasambhava is renowned for his ability to subdue demonic forces and to transform unwholesome activities into Dharma activities wherever his emanation appears. His compassion to help others took him to Tibet, Mongolia, China, Nepal, and all over the world, where he is known by the names of his different manifestations: Padmasambhava, Padmakara, Tsokyi Dorje, Padma Gyalpo, Shakya Senge, Nyima Odzer, Loden Chogsed, Senge Dradog, Dombi Heruka, and Dorje Drolod.

    As the lineage forefather of Tibetan Buddhism, Guru Rinpoche founded the first monastery Samye in Tibet (775CE). Before leaving Tibet, Guru Rinpoche, being aware of the afflictive effect of the kaliyuga (age of decadence) on sentient beings - which causes them to become timid, fearful and bewildered, as a result of the degeneration of life span, environment, perceptions, faculties and increase of negative emotions - prophesied the coming of 108 tertons (treasure revealers).
